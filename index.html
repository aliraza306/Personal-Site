Cuick Media

I founded Cuick Media as a way to connect content creators on Instagram through monetization. Cuick is a collection of over one hundred creators with five million followers among them. Our team at Cuick generated revenue by selling large advertisements to our massive following.

Cuick was one of the original entities to begin large-scale marketing for sports media giant Playline. I ran Cuick for the majority of 2019 before focusing more on my next venture, Flex.

My Background

I began working on social media, primarily Instagram, in the fall of 2017. My good friend and I were growing sports pages for fun, when he received a message from a company called Bullpen Labs. Bullpen (now Wave Sports) wanted to buy his basketball page of approximately thirty thousand followers and pay him monthly to manage it. This was an unthinkable offer for us at the time, and it made clear to me that social media was a real opportunity.
‍
I was very intrigued and immediately found a page of my own, sold it to Wave, and joined their team. At the time, I didn’t even have a personal Instagram page because I saw no value in social media, but this changed my entire mindset towards the medium.  When I left Wave to found my own venture, Cuick, I was managing @dunked (now @buckets) with over eight hundred thousand followers at the time and a team of five content creators that I directed. 
‍
I have done social media advertising campaigns for companies such as Playline and, more recently, TikTok. A personal Instagram page that I grew with my team to over 720,000 organic followers before selling it recently is @gadgetings. Feel free to check it out!
